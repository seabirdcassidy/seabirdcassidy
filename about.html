Hello! Curious to learn more about me and this website?

I spend my time these days as a self-employed computer programmer. I enjoy having the liberty to tunnel into my interests and choose tools that work well for me. I am curious about too many subjects to choose only one for eight hours a day. I feel most energized when I'm learning something new. I want to spend my time deeply understanding things.

I find most corporate environments very stifling, hence my move towards self-employment. I am open to contract work if the project is interesting to me and I see it benefitting the world.

The current version of this website was designed to showcase my book reviews and long-form writing. I was heavily inspired by Swiss Style grids and International Orange. I built this website with Jekyll, a Ruby static site generator, and SASS, a CSS preprocessor. This website also uses D3.js for data visualizations. It is all neatly hosted on GitHub Pages.

Who's the seabird?

The handle "seabirdcassidy" is a reference to one of my favorite songs, "Cassidy", written by John Barlow and Bob Weir of the Grateful Dead. It's a song that I enjoy, not just because of the title, but because of its rolling, bursting with life, upward musical progression. It starts mellow and ends up whirling off, like a bird taking flight. I believe it encompasses a sense of letting go into renewal.

I have seen where the wolf has slept by the silver stream.
I can tell by the mark he left you were in his dream.
Ah, child of countless trees.
Ah, child of boundless seas.
What you are, what you're meant to be
Speaks his name, though you were born to me,
Born to me, Cassidy...

Lost now on the country miles in his Cadillac.

Faring thee well now.

The lyrics "fare thee well" were also used for the name of the last concert tour of the surviving members. Fare Thee Well: Celebrating 50 Years of the Grateful Dead gathered an immense amount of interest. Ticket demand broke the records at Ticketmaster and created absolute mayhem in the secondary ticket market scene. I remember single tickets selling anywhere between $2000 to $15,000, depending on the view of the stage, though tickets dropped as low as $20 days before the shows. With luck, perserverence, and little miracles, my boyfriend and I managed to see each show of the tour. Probably one of the best two weeks of my life!

Bob Weir even said himself that of all the Grateful Dead songs, he wants "Cassidy" played at his memorial service.
